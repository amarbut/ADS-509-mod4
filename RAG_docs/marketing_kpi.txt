Title: Quarterly Marketing KPIs

Overview:
Each quarter, the Marketing team tracks a set of key performance indicators (KPIs) to measure the effectiveness of campaigns and overall strategy. These KPIs cover awareness, engagement, conversion, and retention.

KPI List:
1. Click-Through Rate (CTR) – Percentage of users who click on ads or links after viewing them. Target: 3.0%+ across paid channels.
2. Conversion Rate – Percentage of users who complete a desired action (purchase, signup). Target: 5% on landing pages.
3. Cost Per Acquisition (CPA) – Average spend to acquire a new customer. Target: <$50.
4. Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) – Estimated revenue from a customer over their lifetime. Target: >$400.
5. Social Media Engagement Rate – Likes, shares, and comments relative to followers. Target: 4%+ per campaign.
6. Email Open Rate – Percentage of recipients who open marketing emails. Target: 25%+.
7. Return on Ad Spend (ROAS) – Revenue generated for every dollar spent on advertising. Target: 4x or greater.
8. Net Promoter Score (NPS) – Customer satisfaction and loyalty metric. Target: +40 or higher.
9. Website Bounce Rate – Percentage of visitors leaving without interacting. Target: <40%.
10. Marketing Qualified Leads (MQLs) – Leads deemed ready for sales outreach. Target: 2,000 per quarter.

Usage:
- KPIs are reported weekly to the Marketing Director.
- Monthly summaries highlight trends and areas for optimization.
- Analysts are expected to provide context and narrative with each KPI update.
